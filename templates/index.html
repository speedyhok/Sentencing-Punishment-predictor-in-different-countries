⚖️ Normative Auditing Framework

This auditor implements a consistency and divergence model. It is explicitly designed not to propose a moral baseline, define "the correct punishment," or pass ethical judgment on any legal tradition. Instead, the system utilizes a severity-invariant harm embedding to map crime descriptions into a unified vector space, allowing for direct comparison of sentence scaling behaviors across 20 representative jurisdictions. By measuring relative deviations from the **Reference Median (20 Jurisdictions)**, this system highlights systemic outliers (Within-Jurisdiction Drift) and legal-cultural divergences (Cross-Jurisdiction Drift), maintaining a defensible and transparent stance centered on empirical auditing.
